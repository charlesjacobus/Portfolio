Palette
Over the years, my working process has occasionally yielded what I recognized only after the fact and in an instant something significant and complete in its own right; these are all finished paintings.
1994 - 2001 : oil on handmade wood palette : 5 x 15 inches
The challenge of this series is that the working process remains non-intentional, with respect to any outcome or finished product. In other words, the palette must remain a palette until it is a painting.
Most of these paintings are on ceramic, because of its economy and utility as a working surface. But some, such as this one, are on wood because that was the material at hand — in this case, an old bread board.
1998 - 2000 : oil on wood platter : 12 inches diameter
Because of my working process in which months or years are sometimes spent on a single work, a palette painting may contain some of the tonal character of a different work. In the example above, the Handrail Paintings. And in this latest piece, some of the more recent paintings from A Death in the Family clearly come through.
2021 - 2023 : oil on ceramic plate : 12 inches diameter
